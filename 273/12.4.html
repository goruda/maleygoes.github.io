Examples

Example 1

Find the cross product of \( u=2i+3j+4k \) and \( v = -3i + 5j -8k \) and verify that it is orthogonal to both u and v.

\( u × v = \)
|i j k|
|2 3 4|
|-3 5 -8|
\(= (3 * -8 - 4 * 5)i - (2*-8 - 4 *-3)j + (2 * 5 - 3 * -3)k \)
\( = (-24 - 20)i + (16+12)j + (10 + 9)k \)
\( = -42i + 28j + 19k \)
So the cross product is -42i + 28j + 19k.

How do we know its orthogonal to u or v? We could use the dot product.

Example 2

A boy applies the brakes on a bicycle by applying a downward force of 20 lb on the pedal when the 6-in. crank makes a 40° angle with the horizontal (see the following figure). Find the torque at point P.  

The formula for Torque, Tau, is given by \( \tau = r \times F \) and we know that \( |\tau| = |r \times F| = |r||F|\sin(\theta) \)

The angle between the force applied and the center of the crankshaft is 50° (180-(90+40)). Note 6in = 0.5 feet. So the magnitude of the force must be \( |\tau| = |r \times F| = |0.5||20|\sin(50°) \) = 7.66 ft-lbs of torque.
