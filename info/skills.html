Key Behaviors and Skills for Success in an Asynchronous Online Math Course

Key Behaviors

  • Self-Discipline and Time Management
    • Regular Study Schedule: Establish and adhere to a regular study schedule to stay on track with course materials and deadlines.
    • Avoiding Procrastination: Consistently work on assignments and avoid last-minute cramming.
  • Active Engagement
    • Participating in Discussions: Actively participate in online forums, discussion boards, and peer interactions.
    • Seeking Help: Reach out to instructors or peers when needing clarification or assistance with difficult concepts.
  • Consistent Effort
    • Regular Practice: Practice problems regularly to reinforce understanding and mastery of mathematical concepts.
    • Completion of Assignments: Diligently complete and submit all assignments and assessments on time.
  • Self-Motivation
    • Setting Goals: Set clear, achievable goals for learning and personal progress.
    • Staying Positive: Maintain a positive attitude towards learning and overcoming challenges.

Key Skills

  • Technical Skills
    • Navigating Online Platforms: Proficiency in using the online learning management system (LMS) and other digital tools required for the course.
    • Basic Computer Skills: Comfortable with basic computer operations, such as word processing, spreadsheets, and internet research.
  • Mathematical Skills
    • Foundational Knowledge: A solid understanding of prerequisite mathematical concepts.
    • Problem-Solving Skills: Ability to approach and solve mathematical problems logically and systematically.
  • Communication Skills
    • Effective Writing: Ability to clearly articulate questions, ideas, and solutions in written form.
    • Digital Communication: Proficiency in using email, discussion boards, and other communication tools to interact with instructors and peers.
  • Critical Thinking
    • Analytical Skills: Ability to analyze problems, identify patterns, and apply appropriate mathematical methods.
    • Reflective Thinking: Reflecting on learning processes and outcomes to improve understanding and performance.
  • Organization Skills
    • Keeping Track of Materials: Organizing course materials, notes, and resources in a systematic manner.
    • Monitoring Progress: Regularly checking progress against course objectives and deadlines.

Strategies to Develop These Behaviors and Skills

  • Create a Dedicated Study Space: Set up a quiet, organized space dedicated to studying and completing coursework.
  • Utilize Resources: Take advantage of available resources such as online tutorials, study groups, and office hours.
  • Set Realistic Goals: Break down larger tasks into smaller, manageable goals to maintain motivation and a sense of accomplishment.
  • Develop a Routine: Establish a daily or weekly routine that includes dedicated time for studying and coursework.
  • Reflect and Adapt: Regularly reflect on what study methods are working and what needs to be adjusted to improve learning outcomes.
