Derivative Tests Without The Confusion

When do we care about this?

When we are confronted with a question like "What is the absolute maximum" or "What is the global minimum" of a function \(f(x) \).

What does that mean?

It means we are interested in when the function reaches its peak or valley. When was the revenue the highest? When was the speed the slowest? But we are talking about this in general, so we say "extrema" or "maxima and minima" so that it applies everywhere.

What makes it challenging?

Well, it is really the fact that we are trying to determine this without relying on a graph. So we have to do it algebraically. for the most part.

Things Students Forget On Exams

  • Local means any critical point or endpoint of the interval.
  • Global means the single max or min.
  • TO CHECK ENDPOINTS.
  • You have to check the derivative \( f'(x) \) as well as the height \(f(x)\). So Don't Mix them up!
