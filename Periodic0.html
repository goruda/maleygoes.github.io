Reviewing The Circle

Why do we care about circles and angles?

This will be tough for you to believe. But anything involving Signals (electrical, information) or waves (physics/engineering/life science) will relate to this.

Why? Because anything that repeats is essentially circular. Anything that has rythym or a pattern is circular. And an angle is just how much we've moved around that pattern.

What we'll learn / Objectives

  1. What Radians Are
  2. Important Angles and Terminology of Unit Circle
  3. Why Circles Matter

What you'll need to remember

  • That we move counter-clockwise when we rotate around the circle for the positive direction. This is weird at first.
  • The coordinates on any point in the plane \( (x,y) \) can be also thought of as a triangle, with one corner at (0,0) and at (x,y) (and the other on one of the axes... we just pick x-axis).

Pro Tips

Get Your Hands Dirty / Post Lecture Problem

Try explaining the connection between x, y, \(\sin\theta, \cos\theta\) out loud to yourself. (Talking to yourself is perfectly normal)

Solution

x is the horizontal distance from 0. y is the vertical distance from 0. (x,y) is the rectangular coordinates. \(\cos\theta\) is assigned to x on the unit circle. \(\sin\theta\) is assigned to the y on the unit circle.

Taking It Further

I remember wondering why we would ever care about triangles or circles. On their own, it seems like a waste of time. That is completely wrong. I had never built anything, nor cared about engineering or physics at a high level at the time. THEY MATTER. They come up ALOT.

A big result later on in calculus, is that almost anything that fluctuates can really be modeled with Sine and Cosine (the x and y position going around a circle) as long as we can mess with the parameters. Isn't that nuts?
