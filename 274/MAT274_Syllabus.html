Catalog Description

This course is an introductory survey of ordinary differential equations. First order differential equations and methods for obtaining solutions are investigated. Methods include integration, variation of parameters, and integrating factors. These methods are generalized for second order differential equations. Additional methods include numerical approximation, Laplace Transforms, and power series.

Prerequisites/Co-requisites

MAT 272 with a C- or better

Course Learning Outcomes

  1. Use the language and notation of ordinary differential equations.

  2. Recognize and/or classify various types of ordinary differential equations.

  3. Observe an application situation and apply the appropriate differential model.

  4. Explain the concepts behind various transformations used in differential equations.

  5. Interpret graphically the behavior of solutions to differential equations.

  6. Evaluate results for reasonableness.

Students may vary in their competency levels on these outcomes, and they can expect to achieve these outcomes only if they honor all course policies, attend class regularly, complete all assigned work in good faith and on time, and meet all other course expectations of them as students.

There are 4 exams covering the 4 thematic units of the class. There is no cumulative final exam in this course.

Plagiarism in Math Courses:

There have been a large number of apps, websites, etc. developed in the last 10 or so years to “help” students with their math assignments. Some, at times, are actually helpful. However it is clear that on more procedural problems, they are serving students as an answer generator. Your practice problems are meant to help you understand the concepts, which prepares you for your future courses and assessments (exams). If you lean on tools that aren’t available during assessments you are hamstring-ing yourself to do poorly. You can’t really Google translate yourself through a Spanish course - so I don’t recommend trying that here.
Simply: don’t use these tools unless you are checking your work. If you think you cannot resist the temptation, just refuse using them altogether. Can’t stress that enough. It is often clear as day when they have been used - which means the plagiarism policy will be enforced. See below for Academic Integrity policy.

Instructor Policies & Class Expectations

Attendance and Tardiness

You are expected to attend all scheduled classes, and be on time. In addition, you are responsible for material covered, practice assignments, and announcements made during class whether present or not. I expect you to be prepared for class even if you missed the previous class or could not log on for a certain amount of time. Get in touch with me and/or your classmates if you need to miss a class.
For online students, this means regularly logging in and working on assignments multiple times a week. If this does not happen, you will fall behind, and you can understand why I won’t be lenient with you.
This is the number one piece of advice given between college students – don’t pay for a class you’re not attending!
