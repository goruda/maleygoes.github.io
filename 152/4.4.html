4.4 Applications to Compound Interest

Why We Care

A common and very important application of exponential funtions is to that of interest. We will also use this idea to derive a bigger concept in the next section, which couldn't be more important in calculus and beyond.

Learning about how interest works will arm you to make better financial decisions and understand the numbers that are flying around in the world out there!

What We'll Learn

  • The formula for compound interest. A nominal rate of r%, compounded n times per period, with a initial value of P and time is t: \( B=P(1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt} \)
  • How to model situations, and solve for the different parameters

What do we need to remember to learn this?

  • You will want to remember the form for an exponential function \( f(t)=a(b)^t =a(1+r)^t \) and see why it is equivalent to \( B=P(1+\frac{r}{n})^{nt} \)
  • There are 4 quarters in a year, 52 weeks in a year.
