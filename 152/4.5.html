4.5 The Number e, and Continuous Compounding

Why We Care

The number "e" is ubiquitous. There are a ton of reasons for this, we will go over one or two. But the idea of continuous growth, compounding every instant and in proportion to its present amount - is a pattern we see all over the known world, in all areas of study. So if you do anything in science, you will see "e".

The idea is: what happens if we don't compound weekly or daily, or even hourly... but every instant?? What would happen? Spoiler: This is how we define the growth factor of e. We will get there!

What We'll Learn

  • The natural base e and its applications
  • How to model a continuous compounding situation using \( f(t) = a(e)^{kt} \)
  • a is the initial value, e is e, and k is the continuous growth rate.

What do we need to remember to learn this?

  • You will want to remember the form for an exponential function \( f(t)=a(b)^t =a(1+r)^t \) and try to see why it is equivalent to \( a(e)^{kt} \)
