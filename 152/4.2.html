4.2: Comparing Exponential and Linear Functions

Why We Care

Here we are trying to see a comparison between what we are already quite comfortable with, the linear function, and this other class of function. One grows at a constant, flat rate (linear) and the other grows at a constant percent rate. Because it is in proportion to the present amount! Whereas linear functions don't care how much is present... its just a flat rate ($15/hour, etc.)

What We'll Learn

  • How to identify these functions from tables
  • Finding a formula for \(f(t) = a(b)^t \) when using two data points (this is a huge outcome of the course)
  • Comparing linear versus exponential functions. (Exponentials always beat out linear functions)

What do we need to remember?

  • Linear functions have a flat rate of growth, m: \(f(t) = mt+b\)
  • The constant percent rate of growth will have a constant ratio. For two equally spaced t-values: \(t_1, t_2 \) the fraction \[\frac{f(t_2)}{f(t_1)}\] will have the same value as any other equally spaced inputs.
