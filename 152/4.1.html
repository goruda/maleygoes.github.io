4.1: Intro to Exponential Functions Family

Why We Care

Exponential functions model (meaning, they can represent mathematically) so much that happens in the world. Anything that grows or decays in proportion to the present amount: populations, drugs in the bloodstream, investments, etc. They are WIDELY used to model situations and solve problems.

What We'll Learn

  • Exponential Functions: Functions that have constant percent ROC
  • Formula: \(f(t) = a(b)^t\) where \(b \ge 0 \)
  • a = initial value, b = growth FACTOR.\(b = 1+r) where r is the growth RATE. (What is the difference between a factor and a rate?)
  • Growth is when b < 0, Decay is when \( 0 \le b\le 1 \). As long as a > 0.

What do we need to remember in order to learn this?

  • AROC formula \(\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\)
  • Function notation y=f(t) where y is output, t is input
  • a = initial value, b = growth FACTOR.\(b = 1+r\) where r is the growth RATE. (What is the difference between a factor and a rate?)
  • It may sound silly: but keeping in mind the difference between multiplying, adding, and exponents.
