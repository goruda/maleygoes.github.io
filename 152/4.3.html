4.3 Graphs of Exponential Functions

Why We Care

We focus on the four representations of functions: Words, Formulas, Tables, and Graphs. Understanding the "shape" of a function can help us solve problems from different angles.

REMARK: I have noticed over the years that students who get stuck on a problem involving functions often neglect the graphical perspective when this would often solve their problems. Try to just always check out the graph when you can!

What We'll Learn

  • What the Parameters \(a,b\) mean for the graph of the function \(f(t) = a(b)^t \)
  • Limit Notation. This will carry with us throughout the course.
  • Concavity/Convexity
  • Horizontal asymptotes or long-run trends for exponential functions and how we write that

What do we need to remember?

  • Arrow notation: \(x \to \infty, f(x) \to L \) means "as x gets huge, f(x) gets closer to L"
  • Solving equations graphically. This is a key skill in the class.
  • If you cannot solve it algebraically, graph it! And find where it intersects the value you are interested in.
